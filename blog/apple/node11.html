VisiCalc: The first Killer App

The main thing that turned the tables for the Apple II was not about the computer itself but a program that it supported.

On the 2nd of January 1979, Dan Bricklen and Bob Franskton of the company Software Arts, publish a program called Visicalc. This is the first spreadsheet program for the Apple II and is essentially the first "killer app" of personal computers. The $100 program helped transform personal computers from cool to have toys to essential business products. For the first time businesses started to see computers as an important tool to gain productivity and efficiency. Visicalc was basically an innovative spreadsheet program that could be used for business production planning and calculating finances. No program like it existed before and companies had to buy $2000 Apple II computers just to be able to use it.() Although there was a bit of luck on Apple's part concerning Visicalc. The creator Dan Bricklen only wrote it for Apple II because it was the only computer that was available to him at the time. This program became a huge factor in the sales of the Apple II computer and in 1981, with a combination of great marketing and great luck, the company sold 210,000 units, surpassing the Commodore PET and nearly catching up to the numbers of the TRS-80.
