Some Countries' Economies are tied to Apple

According to the International Monetary Fund, the fate of some national economies is intrinsically tied to the iPhone.
In Ireland for example, where Apple's European operations headquarters are located, iPhone exports alone have contributed to nearly a quarter of the country's economic growth in the year 2017.
There is also South Korea, where Apple suppliers like Samsung/LG are based. Semiconductor exports represent 17.1% of exports and this makes South Korea's economy dependent on how Apple does in the market.()
