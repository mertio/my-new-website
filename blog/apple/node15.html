Finally Success with the Graphical User Interface

The graphical user interface wasn't first seen in Apple's Macintosh. The first computer on the market with a graphical user interface was the Xerox Star in 1981. It was a failure. It was underpowered, slow and overpriced and Xerox never turned a profit from it but it had a great influence on other companies to push for the technology and Apple was one of them.()
Apple's first computer that had a graphical user interface was actually the Apple Lisa(named after Steve Jobs' daughter) in 1983. It was a commercial failure. They intended to sell it to businesses but they did not go for the Lisa because of its high price and they mostly decided to run less expensive IBM PCs, which were already beginning to dominate business desktop computing.() Over 50,000 units were sold by April and an additional 200,000 were sold by the end of the year.
