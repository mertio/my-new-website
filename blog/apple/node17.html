iTunes and the iPod

On the 9th of January in the year 2000, Apple released a music software called iTunes. It was based on another program, SoundJam MP, that Apple had purchased. It was a simple program without a lot of complicated features.


On the 23rd of October, only a month after the 9/11 attacks, Steve Jobs anounced the first iPod as "breakthrough digital device". It had a 1000 song capacity thanks to its 5GB hard drive, but costed $399. The initial reaction towards the product was not good and it was thought to be too expensive for what little it could do.


In July 2002, the 2nd generation iPod was released with 10 to 20GB capacities with a touch sensitive wheel instead of a mechanical one and an all-around better design. And in October 2002, iTunes became compatable with Windows 2000 and XP, causing huge increase in sales. Jobs also gets his friends from the music industry(like Bono from U2 or Mick Jagger) to promote the software.() From then on new models with improvements are released each year and the iPod and iTunes gets bigger and bigger.
