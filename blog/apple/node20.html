The Economics of an iPhone

With the release of the iPhone X with a price tag of $999, it has been the best selling iPhone so far. By 2018, the iPhone makes up approximately 60% of Apple's total revenue. This also makes Apple very dependent on the smartphone market. Below is a graph the shows how much revenue Apple has generated from the iPhone each year.
So how does Apple generate such high revenues? They owe it largely to their sourcing model, where components and materials are gathered from around the world and sometimes even from direct competitors like Samsung. This significantly lowers capital expenses for the company.
