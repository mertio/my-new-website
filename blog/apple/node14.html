The First Macintosh (1984)

In January of 1984, Apple released the Macintosh computer. At first it had only 128K RAM of memory and this as not found to be enough. 8 months later, they released an updated version that had four times as much memory, 512K RAM.
Before the release of the Macintosh, computers were not graphical but text based. You could only operate the computer by typing words onto it. The Macintosh could be used by clicking on pictures (icons) on the screen with a small device called a "mouse". Most computers that are on the market these days operate in this way.()
